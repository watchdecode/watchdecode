## Start with Use Case, Not Hype

Before looking at brands, decide where and how you will wear the watch:

- Office and formal settings
- Daily casual wear
- Active weekends and travel

Your use case should drive your choice of size, water resistance, and strap style.

## The Five Specs That Actually Matter

### 1) Case Size and Lug-to-Lug

Many first-time buyers focus only on case diameter. Lug-to-lug distance is often more important for wrist comfort and visual balance.

### 2) Movement

Look for a movement with broad service support. Well-known calibers from Seiko, Citizen, and Miyota are practical choices for long-term ownership.

### 3) Crystal

Sapphire offers better scratch resistance than mineral crystal. If your budget allows, it is usually worth prioritizing.

### 4) Water Resistance

For most people, **100m water resistance** is a sweet spot for everyday use.

### 5) Strap and Bracelet Quality

A good bracelet elevates daily wear. If the bracelet is weak but the head is strong, plan for a quality aftermarket strap.

## Budget Split Strategy

A practical first-watch budget split:

- 80% on the watch itself
- 20% reserved for strap changes or a bracelet upgrade

This approach gives you flexibility without overspending.
