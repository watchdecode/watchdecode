## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the Tissot PRX Powermatic 80 worth buying over**
**the PRX Quartz in India?**
A: Yes, if your budget reaches ₹57,000–₹63,000. The
Powermatic 80 adds an automatic movement with an
80-hour power reserve, a Nivachron balance spring for
magnetic resistance, and an exhibition caseback. The
price premium over the Quartz version is approximately
₹25,000–₹30,000 at authorised Indian retailers — a
meaningful difference that most buyers who own both
versions describe as fully justified for the mechanical
ownership experience.

**Q: Which Tissot is best for someone starting to collect**
**watches in India under ₹1 lakh?**
A: The PRX Powermatic 80 is the strongest first serious
watch purchase under ₹1 lakh. It covers daily wear,
smart casual, and office settings without compromise,
has strong resale value within the Tissot lineup, and
represents the clearest statement of what modern Tissot
does well — design and movement quality at accessible prices.

**Q: Is the Tissot Seastar 1000 Powermatic 80 Silicium**
**worth the premium over the Quartz version?**
A: For buyers who swim or travel to water destinations
regularly, yes. The automatic Silicium version adds a
silicon balance spring for magnetic resistance and the
satisfaction of a mechanical movement — advantages that
justify the ₹25,000–₹30,000 premium over the quartz
Seastar for active buyers. For urban daily wear where
water activities are rare, the quartz Seastar at ₹34,000–
₹40,000 is the more practical spend.

**Q: Are Tissot watches from grey market sellers safe to**
**buy in India?**
A: Grey market Tissot watches are generally genuine but
carry significant risks in India specifically. Tissot
service centres require proof of authorised purchase for
warranty claims — without it, even genuine watches pay
full service fees. Given that authorised dealer prices
in India are now more competitive following the March
2026 MRP revision, the savings from grey market are
smaller than before while the warranty risk remains.

**Q: How does the Tissot PRX Powermatic 80 compare to**
**the Longines Spirit at similar prices?**
A: They target different buyer priorities. The Longines
Spirit at a similar price offers COSC chronometer
certification, a longer 72-hour power reserve, and
a more traditional watch aesthetic. The PRX Powermatic
80 wins on design — the integrated bracelet is more
distinctive than anything Longines offers at this price.
Buy the Spirit if movement credentials matter most.
Buy the PRX if design is your primary consideration.

more to provide. If Swiss heritage and India service
network matter to you, every watch on this list
